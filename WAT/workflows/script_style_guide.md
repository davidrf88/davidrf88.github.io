# Script Style Guide — "Bar Bet" Rewrite Rules

This guide defines the voice, tone, and structure for every video script produced by the WAT framework. After writing the initial draft (Step 5), the agent must run a **rewrite pass** using these rules before saving.

---

## 1. The "Bar Bet" Rule

Write every script as if you're telling a friend a crazy story at a bar or over coffee.

- **Test:** If it sounds too formal to say to a friend, it's too formal for the video.
- **Avoid:** "furthermore," "technically," "classified as" — unless used for comedic effect.
- **Use instead:** "basically," "turns out," "here's the wild part."

---

## 2. The 3-Second Hook

The first sentence must challenge a "common truth" or ask a question the viewer didn't know they had.

- **Formula:** [Common Belief] + [The Twist]
- **Example:** "You think avocados are veggies? Think again."

---

## 3. "Show, Don't Just Tell" — Metaphor Over Jargon

If a technical term is necessary, immediately anchor it to a common object.

- **Rule:** Never use a botanical, scientific, or technical term without comparing it to something in a kitchen, a grocery store, or everyday life.
- **Example:** Instead of "monocarpous ovary," say "it grows from a single flower part."

---

## 4. Write for the Ear, Not the Eye

People listen to videos; they don't read them.

- **Use short, punchy sentences.** Vary the length to create rhythm.
- **The breath test:** Read the script out loud. If you run out of breath or stumble, break that sentence in two.

---

## 5. The "Value-Linked" Outro

Don't just ask for a sub — tell them *why* they need it based on what they just watched.

- **Rule:** Every CTA must bridge the content to the subscription.
- **Template:** "I post [Category of Fact] like this every day. Subscribe so you don't miss the next [Specific Benefit]."

---

## Quick-Reference Table

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Voice** | First-person and active ("I found out" vs. "It was discovered") |
| **Pacing** | No sentence longer than 15 words |
| **Jargon** | Max 1 technical term per 30 seconds (explained immediately) |
| **Vibe** | "Did you know?" energy, not "Today we will learn" energy |

---

## How to Apply

After writing the initial script draft, do a **second pass** (Step 5b) where you:

1. **Hook check** — Does the first line use [Common Belief] + [Twist]? Rewrite if not.
2. **Bar test** — Read every sentence. Would you say this at a bar? Replace formal words.
3. **Sentence scan** — Any sentence over 15 words? Split it.
4. **Jargon audit** — More than 1 technical term per 30 sec? Cut or anchor with a metaphor.
5. **Voice flip** — Any passive voice? Flip to first-person active.
6. **Outro check** — Does the last line use the value-linked CTA template? Add it.
7. **Breath test** — Read out loud. Fix anything you stumble on.
